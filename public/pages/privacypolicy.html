2) Scope & Applicability

This Policy applies to personal data we process when you use AidSync websites, apps, and services (collectively, the "Services"). It does not cover third-party services linked from our platform.

3) Key Definitions

  • Personal Data: Information identifying or reasonably capable of identifying an individual.
  • Processing: Any operation performed on personal data.
  • Controller/Processor: Entity determining purposes/means of processing; entity processing on behalf of a controller.

4) Data We Collect

  • Account Data: name, age, email, username, password (hashed), role/type, preferences.
  • Usage/Device Data: IP address, identifiers, browser/device details, pages viewed, actions, timestamps, diagnostic logs.
  • Content You Provide: messages, forms, uploads (e.g., profile photos), and support requests.
  • From Third Parties: identity or security providers, analytics, and anti-fraud partners.
  • Sensitive Data: only if you choose to provide it. We ask that you avoid sharing sensitive data unless necessary.

5) Cookies & Tracking

We use essential cookies to operate the Services, and with consent, analytics/advertising technologies to measure usage and improve experience. You can adjust preferences in your browser settings. Where required, we will present a consent banner.

6) How We Use Data & Legal Bases

  • Provide and operate the Services; create and manage accounts (Contract/GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Secure, monitor, prevent abuse/fraud, and troubleshoot (Legitimate Interests/Art. 6(1)(f); Legal Obligation/Art. 6(1)(c)).
  • Communicate with you about updates and features (Legitimate Interests/Consent).
  • Personalize and improve the Services, run analytics (Legitimate Interests/Consent).
  • Comply with law and enforce terms (Legal Obligation/Legitimate Interests).

7) Sharing & Disclosure

We share personal data only as necessary to provide our Services or comply with law:

  • Service Providers/Processors: hosting, storage, authentication, analytics, communications, and security vendors under DPAs.
  • Business Transfers: in the event of a merger, acquisition, or asset sale.
  • Legal: to comply with lawful requests or protect rights, safety, and property.

We do not sell your personal data. We may share de-identified or aggregated data.

8) International Transfers

If data is transferred outside your country, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses (and UK Addendum where applicable), and we assess local laws and implement supplementary measures when needed.

9) Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described, including legal, accounting, or reporting obligations. Criteria include account status, legal requirements, and dispute resolution needs.

10) Security

We use administrative, technical, and organizational measures appropriate to the risk, including encryption in transit, access controls, monitoring, and regular reviews. No method is 100% secure; please protect your credentials.

11) Your Rights

EU/EEA/UK (GDPR/UK GDPR)

Access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection. You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior processing. Lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority.

California (CCPA/CPRA)

Right to know, delete, correct, and opt-out of sale/sharing of personal information. We do not sell personal information. Non-discrimination for exercising rights.

Brazil (LGPD)

Confirmation of processing, access, correction, anonymization/blocking/deletion, data portability, information on sharing, and withdrawal of consent.

Canada (PIPEDA)

Access, correction, and complaint rights via the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada.

To exercise rights, email privacy@aidsync.example. We will verify your identity and respond within the timelines required by law.

12) Children
